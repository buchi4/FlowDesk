# FlowDesk — Slack Agent for Good

Slack is where work happens, but the constant stream of messages has turned it into a source of cognitive overload. Information gets lost. Commitments vanish into the scrollback. Endless notifications create anxiety instead of productivity.

FlowDesk was built to solve this exact problem without forcing users to change their behavior.

## The Problem
When someone says "I'll handle that by Friday" in a busy channel, three things usually happen:
1. No one writes it down.
2. The sender forgets.
3. The requester has to nervously follow up next week.

This creates friction, mistrust, and "notification fatigue" as people constantly check channels to make sure they didn't miss something.

## The FlowDesk Impact
FlowDesk acts as an invisible Chief of Staff. 
- It watches the conversation natively and catches commitments *as they happen*. 
- It reduces anxiety by giving you a clear `/mytasks` list of exactly what you promised to do.
- It reduces noise by providing a `/summary` and a Daily Digest, meaning you don't have to read 500 messages in `#general` just to find out what happened.
- It democratizes knowledge by allowing anyone to `/ask` questions about past decisions, ending the cycle of relitigating the same arguments because no one can find the original Slack thread.

By reducing cognitive load and preventing dropped balls, FlowDesk makes remote and hybrid work healthier, more accountable, and less stressful.
